Franklin's Thirteen Virtues, kept daily.

In 1726, Benjamin Franklin devised a system for his own moral improvement: thirteen virtues, one focus each week, a small notebook for marking each day. He practiced it, off and on, for the rest of his life.

"I was surpris'd to find myself so much fuller of faults than I had imagined; but I had the satisfaction of seeing them diminish."
— Autobiography, 1791

VirtueKeeper is a quiet, mobile version of that notebook. A mirror, not a plan. No ads, no feeds, no scoring.
